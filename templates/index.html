Project Objective & Architecture

The goal of this project is to develop an end-to-end predictive machine learning model to estimate the champion of the FIFA World Cup 2026. Under the new tournament format, 48 teams will participate. By collecting, preprocessing, and engineering features for teams, players, and team managers, we train classification models to predict match-level outcomes and execute a tournament-wide Monte Carlo simulation to find the overall winning probabilities.

Machine Learning Workflow

1

Data Collection (Team, Player & Manager Stats)

Collects raw historical World Cup data, squad ratings, ages, market values, and managers' win percentages to capture a comprehensive portrait of team strength.

2

Feature Engineering (Difference Modeling & Scaling)

Instead of raw values, matches are represented by symmetric relative differences (e.g., rank_diff = Team A Rank - Team B Rank) and standardized using StandardScaler to allow gradient models to converge efficiently.

3

Model Training & Validation

Compares Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting Classifiers. Uses 5-Fold Cross-Validation on the historical matches to guard against overfitting.

4

Tournament Simulation (Monte Carlo Simulation)

Uses the trained model's match win/loss predictions to run 1,000 simulations of the actual 2026 group stage and knockout bracket, aggregating frequencies to map team-level winning probabilities.
